# Game Development


I am passionate about gaming to create interesting trials and challenges for people to enjoy.


I like tech art and 3d modelling, they allow a view that is impossible in reality to be visualized.


I enjoy designing and implementing gameplay systems, which forms the laws of a digital universe where new actions and reactions are possible.


To date, I created a number of prototypes and game jam projects to refine my game development skills. Most of them are available on my [itch.io profile](https://tinygodzilla.itch.io/).


Although the majority of these projects are 2D, I am looking to focus my attention on 3D game creation.

As a part of my game development journey, I also do some observation on existing game systems to understand their implementation and nuances. You can find the case studies [here](https://github.com/TinyZilla/docs_mechanic_analysis).

## Game Engines
I am familiar with a number of game engines:


### Unity
I have the most professional experience with Unity. Marvel Contest of Champions is a mobile game that I've worked on most recently, handling most of the network calls to interface with the game services. During my time at Ctrl V I also helped create their virtual lobby area which players load in and launch games from in a networked environment.


Overall I am familiar with the Unity capabilities and scripting tools.


### Unreal


I've worked with Unreal during one of my internships at [Rave](https://rave.io/). They were looking to create a VR client for mobile devices that runs with Samsung Gear VR. I used a combination of C++ and Blueprint scripting to create interactable user interfaces in an VR environment.


### Godot


In my opinion Godot is a very capable 2D game engine. I've created various personal projects and game jams with the engine, and I have explored the majority of the bells and whistles that the engine has to offer.


I enjoyed the ease of use of GDScript, wrote plenty of Godot Shader materials, and created various particle effects.


### Bevy


I am currently experimenting with Bevy with my personal projects. It is not very mature and doesn't even have an editor. But its ECS framework is very intuitive for someone who came from a back-end background and worked a decent amount in the context of databases.


Rust is also a language I find increased fondness towards, so I am working with this engine to explore its capabilities at the moment.
